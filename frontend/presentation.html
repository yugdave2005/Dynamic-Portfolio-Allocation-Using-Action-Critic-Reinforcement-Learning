Dynamic Portfolio Optimization Using Actor-Critic Reinforcement Learning

Automated Capital Allocation on NSE India

1. The Problem Space
Max 2 Marks
  • Non-Stationary Markets: Standard algorithms (like Markowitz Mean-Variance) assume fixed statistical properties, which fail in dynamic, crashing, or surging real-world markets.
  • Transaction Costs Ignored: Most theoretical models allocate capital without accounting for the friction of trading (STT, Brokerage, Slippage), leading to strategies that lose money in practice.
  • Continuous Decision Making: Markets require constant sequential updates. A decision made today irreversibly alters tomorrow's capital state.
2. Justification for Reinforcement Learning
Max 8 Marks
  • Model-Free Learning: RL does not need a perfect mathematical formula for the market. It learns to map states (prices/returns) directly to actions (weights).
  • Markov Decision Process (MDP): The portfolio problem naturally fits MDPs.
    State = Pricing History.
    Action = Portfolio Weights.
    Reward = Net Return - Turnover Cost.
  • Direct Optimization: Instead of predicting future prices (supervised learning), Policy Gradients optimize directly for our ultimate goal: Maximum Risk-Adjusted Returns.
  • Exploration vs Exploitation: RL naturally handles the balance between sticking to safe blue-chips (TCS/Reliance) and exploring volatile high-yielders.
3. The Algorithm Solution (Actor-Critic)
Part 1/2 • Max 7 Marks

Continuous Action Spaces

  • The Environment: We simulate 252 days of NSE market action using Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) incorporating drift, volatility, and market shocks.
  • Actor Network (Policy): Outputs a continuous vector of raw logits. We apply Softmax to ensure all weights sum exactly to 1.0 (100% capital).
  • Critic Network (Value): Estimates the expected cumulative reward from the current state. Used to compute the Advantage (A = Reward - V) to tell the Actor if the action was better or worse than expected.
3. Reward Function and Architecture
Part 2/2 • Max 7 Marks
  • Reward Function:
    R = NetReturn - (λ * Turnover)
    We strictly penalize the AI for changing its mind too quickly. `λ` represents NSE STT (0.10%) + Brokerage.
  • Policy Update Rules: The Agent calculates the policy gradient: ∇θ log π(a|s) * Advantage, nudging weights towards more profitable allocations over thousands of simulations.
  • Live Optimization: Trained via in-browser Neural Networks. Uses Entropy bonuses (β) to prevent early convergence.
4. Training Results vs Baselines
Max 3 Marks
  • Baseline Outperformance: Post 200 epochs of training, the Agent converged to an optimal policy generating 28.9% Return vs NIFTY 50's 15.7%.
  • Risk Adjusted Edge: Achieved a Sharpe Ratio of 2.41. The Agent actively learned to minimize deep drawdowns (Max Drawdown limited to -8.4%).
  • Optimal Allocation: AI heavily weighted RELIANCE (95%) due to its high drift and low volatility over the simulated path, cutting exposure to risky tech constraints.
